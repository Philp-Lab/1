Dr Gareth Fletcher

Research Officer

Background

Gareth completed a PhD at the University of Birmingham (2012-2016) in the field of exercise metabolism and substrate oxidation. More specifically, Garethâ€™s thesis was concerned with the determinants of the inter-individual variability in fat oxidation during exercise, and the impact of over-feeding and high fat diets on substrate oxidation and endurance performance.


Gareth then worked at GlaxoSmithKline Health Care as a Post-Doc Research Scientist within the Human Performance Lab (HPL). This was a varied role, with tasks ranging from undertaking physiological testing of elite world class athletes through to designing research study protocols, developing procedures and documents to managing and delivering clinical R&D trials to support GSK global brands.



Joining the Garvan Institiute of Medical Research in July 2018, Gareth works within the Mitochondrial Metabolism & Ageing group. Here, he is responsible for leading the human clinical trials concerning nutraceutical compounds of interest to the group.


Outside of the work Gareth is a keen Triathlete and can regularly be seen donning his lycra, dodging the vicious swooping Magpie atacks and avoiding the sharks when out for a swim in the sea!

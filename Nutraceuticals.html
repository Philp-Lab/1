Ursolic Acid

Ursolic acid (UA) is a natural food-derived nutrient (found in many herbs such as Rosemary or Oregano, and also found in the peel of waxy fruits such as apples.

Why are we interested in this molecule?

Through mRNA gene expression analysis, Ursolic Acid was recently identified out as a nutritional compound with a genetic profile opposite to that of muscle atrophy-inducing stresses such as fasting or spinal cord injury (Kunkel et al 2011, Cell Met, 13, 6, P627-638).

Ursolic Acid has been shown in mice to not only prevent muscle wasting seen during periods of fasting but to also increase the size and strength of muscles without the need for strength training. We are therefore interested to study this compound in more detail and see if the exciting findings can be replicated in humans.

Vitamin D

Also know as the sunshine vitamin because humans are capable of synthesising it through UV exposure, we also obtain it through our diet.

Why are we interested in this vitamin?

Although traditionaly though to just have an important role in calcium & mineral homeostasis (bone health), vitamin D is now thought to be much more important. Emerging evidence shows this molecule or its many derivatives have other biological activity with potential to exert broad-ranging effects on metabolism and health. These effects range from modulating immune responses to diabetes to skeletal muscle function. 
The presence of the vitamin D receptor (VDR) throughout almost every cell in the body highlights the potential for it to impact metabolism.

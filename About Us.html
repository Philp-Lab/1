The Mitochondrial Metabolism and Ageing laboratory was recently set up at the Gavan Medical Research Institute in Sydney.


With leading facilities and capabilities the group aim to ............


Keep an eye out for our publications and be sure to follow the lab on twitter for updates on current work and news 


Our group is broadly interested in gaining a better understanding of the molecular signals and pathways of ageing. The impact of aging on metabolism, or vice versa the impact of metabolism on aging. Does mitochondrial dysregulation drive an aging phenotype? Or is it aging per se that is driving mitochondiral disfunction?

Neutracueticals describes... there are many potentially exciting neutraceuticles  available that are thought to prevent losses in muscle and improve metabolic health. For instance we are currently investigating the impact of a plant derived neutracuetical that has been shown to prevent muscle wasting typically seen during periods of fasting or inactivity. For further information on this study please contact our research officer Gareth Fletcher for further details.

31st October 2018

Al & Val Rosenstrauss Fellowship

Dr Andy Philp has recently been awarded the Al & Val Rosenstrauss Fellowship, generously provided by The Rebecca L. Cooper Medical Research Foundation.

Using this fellowship, Dr Philp and his research group, aim to harness the effects of exercise into therapies for diseases of ageing. We aim to identify signal transduction pathways that are important in metabolic control of skeletal muscle, using this molecular blueprint to develop exercise, dietary, nutraceutical and pharmaceutical therapies to modulate healthspan.

The Al & Val Rosenstrauss Fellowship provides an opportunity to extend existing research projects into a more clinically focused direction. The Fellowship will provide $80,000 per year, for four years. With this support, Dr Philp will study the relationship between reduced mitochondrial function and the onset of sarcopenia, and test whether increasing mitochondrial function with BGP-15, a small pharmaceutical compound, can prevent the onset of sarcopenia, with the goal of finding a viable treatment strategy to combat sarcopenia, and promote healthy ageing
